A small studio. A long memory.

We've been the same kind of builder for 26 years — small team, long timelines, signed-off by hand. This is our story, and the people behind it.

Our story

It started with one home
for one family.

In 1998, our founder Devashri Mehta sold her grandmother's silver to fund the construction of a single villa in Aundh. She supervised the masons herself, sourced teak from Karnataka, and lived on-site for nine months.

That first home is still standing — and still owned by the same family. They send us photos every Diwali. That's the moment the studio's philosophy was set: build like you'll have to walk past it every day for the next thirty years.

Today we are still small — 38 people, never more — and we still build only six to eight projects at any given time. The discipline of constraint is what lets us obsess over the work.

The long road

A timeline,
told without hurry.

Five moments that shaped how we build today — none of them are awards.

1998

The first stone

Devashri Mehta builds her first home in Aundh, Pune — a single villa, three bedrooms, hand-finished. Sold to the family that still owns it today.

2006

Mumbai expansion

The studio opens a satellite office in Bandra-Kurla, taking on its first commercial project — Devashri Heights. The first time the team grew past 12 people.

2012

The pause

After a tough year delivering three projects in parallel, the studio commits to never running more than eight active projects at once. A discipline still held today.

2018

IGBC Gold across the board

Every devashri project — old and new — is retrofitted or certified to IGBC Gold sustainability standards. A multi-year, self-funded effort.

2024

Three corridors, one studio

The Bangalore studio reaches 10 completed projects. We remain 38 people, hand-picked, and we still deliver every project on time.

What we hold

Three things we
refuse to compromise.

Everything else is up for discussion. These three are the spine of the studio.

01 / Material

Honest material, honest finish

We don't laminate, we don't simulate. If it looks like marble, it is marble. If it looks like teak, it is teak.

02 / Time

Time as a tool, not a constraint

A villa takes 16 months. An apartment block takes 28. We do not accelerate to win business, ever.

03 / Trust

Show the books, always

Every owner gets quarterly construction reports, escrow statements, and named-supplier invoices. No black boxes.
